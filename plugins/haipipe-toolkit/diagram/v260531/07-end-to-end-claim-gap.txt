🧭  End-to-end workflow — ask → claim-gap → probe → K/W → cash-out
========================================================================

  This diagram names the hinge between 📖 narrative-cycle and 🔧 probe-cycle:

      Claim Gap Contract

  A narrative does not vaguely "run more experiments". It exposes a claim
  slot whose evidence is missing or weak. That slot becomes a contract for
  one probe-cycle. The probe returns 🟨 K (what is now known) and 🟧 W
  (what should happen next). The narrative then re-reads K/W and asks
  🔥 ignite?

─§ Structure rule ──────────────────────────────────────────

  The hierarchy is expressed by REFERENCES, never by nesting.

  narratives/N01_*/claims.md     owns C-slots + gap contracts
  probes/P.A03_*/probe.yaml      owns the focused reality test
  tasks/.../metrics.json         owns measurements
  insights/K_knowledge/K07.md    owns the settled claim returned to narrative
  insights/W_wisdom/W04.md       owns the next move returned to narrative

  Implementation note: K is wired now; W is the next wiring target. The
  end-to-end contract is still K + W because narrative needs both the claim
  and the next move to decide ignite.

  📌 One sentence:

  A full end-to-end turn is an ask becoming a story, a story exposing a
  claim gap, the claim gap becoming a probe contract, the probe filing K/W,
  and the story deciding whether it is now ignited enough to cash out.
