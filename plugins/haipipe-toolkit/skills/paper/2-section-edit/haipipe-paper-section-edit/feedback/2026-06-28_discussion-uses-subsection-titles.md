MISQ Discussion sections use substantive topic-specific subsection titles (not "no subsection titles"). This was discovered via a background subagent survey of 10 MISQ papers (Kitchens 2020, Zhang & Xu 2021, 8 papers from MISQ Sep 2024 meta-analysis).

Key norms for MISQ Discussion:
- Subsection titles are substantive: "Implications for Conceptualizing Echo Chambers" not "Theoretical Implications"
- Contributions are enumerated: "First... Second... Third..."
- Each contribution paragraph connects finding back to prior literature
- Practical implications come AFTER research contributions, never before
- Each limitation paragraph PAIRS one limitation with one future research direction
- Standalone Conclusion paragraph is optional (some papers have it, some don't)

The editing skill should encode these as Discussion-specific scaffold rules when the venue is MISQ/UTD-IS. The "no subsection titles" initial plan was wrong for Discussion (though it was correct for Theory per Ritu's "too many subsections" feedback, which applied to §3 not §7).
