# JAMA-family Style Profile (imitate this)

Distilled language style and preferences to imitate when writing or editing a
JAMA-family manuscript. This is a STARTER from JAMA-family conventions; ENRICH it
from the actual papers stored in `exemplars/` as they are added (pull real
sentences and mirror their shapes).

## Abstract

- Structured: Importance / Objective / Design, Setting, Participants / Exposures /
  Main Outcomes and Measures / Results / Conclusions and Relevance.
- Results sentence shape: "X was associated with Y (effect size; 95% CI; P)."
- Conclusions and Relevance: 1-2 sentences, the clinical or policy takeaway, cautious.

## Sentences

- Short, declarative, one finding each. Past tense for results.
- Associational verbs only for observational data ("was associated with"); never
  causal ("caused", "led to", "reduced") unless from a trial.
- Effect sizes in clinical units with 95% CI; define abbreviations on first use;
  minimal jargon (no econ terms like "extensive/intensive margin").

## Results

- Lead with the cohort (Table 1), then the primary outcome, then secondary / subgroup.
- One result per sentence; numbers + CIs; no interpretation (that belongs in Discussion).

## Discussion

- Opens with a concise statement of principal findings: "In this [design] of [N]
  [population], [primary finding]."
- Then: comparison to prior work -> a plausible mechanism (a trade-off, not blame) ->
  a dedicated Limitations paragraph (observational, confounding, generalizability) ->
  Conclusions / clinical relevance.

## Tone & preferences

- Measured, patient-centered, policy-aware; no hype, no buzzword stacks.
- Title pattern: "Association of [exposure] With [outcome] Among [population]".
- Reporting: STROBE (observational); structured abstract; CONSORT (trials).
