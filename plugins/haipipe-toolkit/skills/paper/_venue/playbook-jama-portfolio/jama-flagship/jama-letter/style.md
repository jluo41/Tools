# JAMA Flagship Research Letter -- Section Style Guide

Extracted from JAMA Research Letter exemplars. Supplements `style-profile.md`.

Research Letters are brief, standalone research communications in JAMA. They are NOT responses to published articles. They present original data in a compressed format.

## Word budget

- **600-800 words** total (body text only, excluding references, tables, and figure legends). JAMA's author instructions specify a maximum of 600 words for research letters, though published letters often run slightly longer.
- Yang (2026): ~750w body text. Cantor (2025): ~700w body text.
- References: maximum 6.
- Figures/Tables: maximum 1 Figure OR 1 Table (some letters include both a Figure and a Table if space permits). Multi-panel figures are common.
- No abstract. No Key Points box.

## Structure

Research Letters have NO section headings in the traditional sense. Instead, they use inline labels:

```
[untitled opening paragraphs: background + gap + objective]
Methods | [methods text]
Results | [results text]
Discussion | [discussion text]
```

The labels "Methods |", "Results |", and "Discussion |" appear as bold inline markers (with a pipe character), not as section headings. The opening paragraphs have no label.

## Arc

```
clinical problem + prevalence (1-2 sentences)
  -> gap: what is not known (1-2 sentences)
  -> this study: what we examined (1 sentence)
  -> Methods | data source, population, measures, analysis (1-2 paragraphs)
  -> Results | key findings with numbers (1-2 paragraphs)
  -> Discussion | interpretation + limitations + implication (1 paragraph)
```

## Signature moves

1. **Open cold with the clinical problem.** No pleasantries. The first sentence states the issue with a concrete fact.
   - "For decades, clinical guidelines have discouraged use of high-risk central nervous system (CNS)-active medications because they increase risk of delirium, falls, and hospitalizations, particularly for older adults with cognitive impairment." [Yang 2026]
   - "The COVID-19 pandemic placed significant strain on the US health care workforce. Physicians experienced a sudden change to usual practice activities, with practice interruptions peaking in April 2020." [Cantor 2025]

2. **Transition to objective in 2-3 sentences.** After stating the problem, the letter names what this study examined.
   - "This study examined prescribing patterns of potentially inappropriate CNS-active medications among older adults with and without cognitive impairment no dementia (CIND) and dementia from 2013 to 2021." [Yang 2026]
   - "We characterized trends in physician participation in the Medicare program between 2013 and 2023 and determined whether physician- and county-level factors were associated with program exit." [Cantor 2025]

3. **Methods | is compressed but complete.** Names data source, population definition, time window, variables, analytic approach, reporting standard, and software. All in 1-2 dense paragraphs.
   - "Methods | We used the Health and Retirement Study (HRS) linked to Medicare fee-for-service claims from January 1, 2013, to December 31, 2021. ... We followed the STROBE reporting guidelines. ... Analyses used SAS 9.4 (SAS Institute). Two-sided P < .05 indicated statistical significance." [Yang 2026]
   - "Methods | We used the 100% sample of Medicare FFS claims data... Physician demographic, specialty, and geographic information came from the Medicare Data on Provider Practice and Specialty and the National Plan & Provider Enumeration System. ... The study was approved by Brown University's review board and followed Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology (STROBE) reporting guideline... All analyses were completed using Stata version 18 (StataCorp)." [Cantor 2025]

4. **Results | packs many numbers into few sentences.** Each sentence carries a specific finding with its CI and P value. Trends stated as "from X% to Y%."
   - "Results | We identified 4842 participants from 2013 to 2021 (Table). ... In adjusted analyses, 19.9% (95% CI, 18.1%-21.6%) of beneficiaries in 2013 received 1 or more potentially inappropriate CNS-active prescriptions, declining to 16.2% (95% CI, 14.3%-18.1%) in 2021, a 3.7-percentage point decrease (P = .003)." [Yang 2026]
   - "Results | Between 2013 and 2023, there were 11.6 billion claims for physician services from more than 960 000 unique physicians. Over this time, the number of physicians participating in the Medicare program increased by 6.3%, from 585 642 to 622 342, with differing trends in numbers by physician age, sex, specialty, and metropolitan location and HPSA status (Table 1)." [Cantor 2025]

5. **Discussion | is one paragraph, 3-5 sentences.** Restates the key finding, names 1-2 limitations briefly, and closes with an implication.
   - "Discussion | Despite decades of guidelines cautioning against their use, many older adults continue to receive potentially inappropriate CNS-active medications. ... Study limitations include unavailable Medicare Advantage data, potentially missing clinical information, and the focus of analyses on prescribing prevalence vs cumulative exposure." [Yang 2026]
   - "Discussion | We characterized trends in the number of physicians participating in the Medicare program from 2013 to 2023 and identified physician- and county-level characteristics associated with program exit. ... This study has several limitations. Our analyses were restricted to Medicare FFS data and do not account for physician participation in Medicare Advantage, Medicaid, or commercial insurance." [Cantor 2025]

6. **Reporting standard still cited.** Even research letters cite STROBE for observational studies.

7. **Multi-panel Figure is the standard display.** Yang (2026) uses a 3-panel Figure (A: trends by cognition, B: trends by drug class, C: trends by appropriateness). Cantor (2025) uses 2 Tables (descriptive + regression).

## Exemplar sentences (shape, not content)

**Opening statement**:
- "For decades, [guidelines/evidence] have [recommended/discouraged] [practice] because [clinical rationale]." [Yang 2026]
- "The [event/trend] placed significant strain on [system]. [Actors] experienced [consequence]." [Cantor 2025]

**Study objective**:
- "This study examined [patterns/trends/associations] of [exposure] among [population] from [year] to [year]." [general pattern]

**Methods data source**:
- "We used the [dataset name] linked to [claims/survey] from [date range]." [general pattern]

**Results trend**:
- "In adjusted analyses, [X]% (95% CI, [L]%-[U]%) of [population] in [year] [had outcome], [declining/increasing] to [Y]% (95% CI, [L]%-[U]%) in [year], a [Z]-percentage point [decrease/increase] (P = [value])." [general pattern]

**Discussion closer**:
- "Study limitations include [brief list]. [Implication for policy/practice]." [general pattern]

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT include an abstract or Key Points box. Research letters have neither.
- Do NOT use formal section headings (Methods, Results, Discussion as H2/H3). Use inline markers: "Methods |", "Results |", "Discussion |".
- Do NOT exceed 6 references.
- Do NOT include more than 1 Figure and 1 Table combined (check current JAMA guidelines; the norm is 1 display total, but some letters carry 2).
- Do NOT write a multi-paragraph Discussion. It is always 1 paragraph.
- Do NOT skip the reporting standard citation (STROBE) even in a letter.
- Do NOT include a detailed statistical methods description. Compress to 1-2 sentences naming the approach and software.
- Do NOT include separate Limitations or Conclusions subsections. Both are folded into the single Discussion paragraph.
- Do NOT preview findings in the opening paragraphs. State the objective only.

## Paragraph structure

| Block | Paragraphs | Job |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Opening (unlabeled) | 1-2 | Clinical problem + gap + objective |
| Methods (pipe label) | 1-2 | Data source, population, measures, analysis, reporting standard |
| Results (pipe label) | 1-2 | Key findings with numbers, CI, P; reference Table/Figure |
| Discussion (pipe label) | 1 | Restate finding + 1-2 limitations + implication |

Total: 5-7 paragraphs, ~600-800 words.
