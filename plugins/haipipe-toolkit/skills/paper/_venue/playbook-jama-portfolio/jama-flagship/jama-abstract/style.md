# JAMA Flagship Abstract -- Section Style Guide

Extracted from JAMA Original Investigation exemplars. Supplements `style-profile.md`.

## Word budget

- ~350 words for the structured abstract body (all labeled fields combined). JAMA allows up to 350 words for Original Investigations.
- Krebs (2018): ~340w. Mathioudakis (2025): ~350w. Cipriani (2026): ~340w. Rotenstein (2026): ~280w.
- RCTs also carry a TRIAL REGISTRATION line below the abstract (not counted in word limit).

## Key Points box

JAMA Original Investigations include a **Key Points** box (displayed in a sidebar or callout), separate from the abstract. Three labeled one-liners:

- **Question**: One sentence framing the clinical question. Starts with an interrogative.
  - "For patients with moderate to severe chronic back pain or hip or knee osteoarthritis pain despite analgesic use, does opioid medication compared with nonopioid medication result in better pain-related function?" [Krebs 2018]
  - "How does referral to a lifestyle intervention exclusively driven by artificial intelligence (AI) compare with referral to a human coach-led Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) lifestyle intervention?" [Mathioudakis 2025]
  - "How is adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) scribes associated with changes in electronic health record (EHR) time expenditure and weekly visit volume?" [Rotenstein 2026]
- **Findings**: "In this [design] [including/of] [N] [population], [key result with numbers]."
  - "In this randomized clinical trial that included 240 patients, the use of opioid vs nonopioid medication therapy did not result in significantly better pain-related function over 12 months (3.4 vs 3.3 points on an 11-point scale at 12 months, respectively)." [Krebs 2018]
  - "In this randomized clinical trial involving 368 adults with overweight or obesity and prediabetes, 31.7% of participants randomized to referral to an AI-led DPP and 31.9% of participants randomized to referral to a human-led DPP group achieved the primary composite outcome..." [Mathioudakis 2025]
  - "In 5 academic medical centers, AI scribe adoption was associated with decreases in total EHR and documentation time of 13.4 and 16.0 minutes, respectively, and an increase of 0.49 visits per week." [Rotenstein 2026]
- **Meaning**: One sentence, cautious implication. Starts with "This study..." or "These findings..." or "Among [population]..."
  - "This study does not support initiation of opioid therapy for moderate to severe chronic back pain or hip or knee osteoarthritis pain." [Krebs 2018]
  - "Among adults with prediabetes and overweight or obesity, a fully automated AI-led DPP may be a viable alternative to a DPP led by human coaches." [Mathioudakis 2025]
  - "These findings indicate initial, moderately beneficial associations of AI scribes with changes in key EHR activity measures and visit volume." [Rotenstein 2026]

## Structured abstract fields

Fields appear in this exact order, each as a bold red label followed by inline text (not a new paragraph):

### For RCTs (CONSORT):
1. **IMPORTANCE** (1-2 sentences, ~30w)
2. **OBJECTIVE** (1 sentence, ~20-30w, starts with "To...")
3. **DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS** (~50-60w)
4. **INTERVENTIONS** (~40-60w, for RCTs) / **EXPOSURES** (~20-30w, for observational)
5. **MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES** (~40-60w)
6. **RESULTS** (~100-120w, the longest field)
7. **CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE** (~30-40w)

### For observational studies (STROBE):
Same fields, but INTERVENTIONS becomes **EXPOSURES**, and DESIGN label may read "DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS" with the study design named (cohort, cross-sectional, etc.).

## Arc

```
clinical problem + evidence gap (IMPORTANCE)
  -> what this study does (OBJECTIVE)
  -> how: design, setting, population, timeframe (DESIGN...)
  -> what was tested/compared (INTERVENTIONS/EXPOSURES)
  -> how outcomes were measured (MAIN OUTCOMES...)
  -> what happened, with numbers (RESULTS)
  -> what it means, cautiously (CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE)
```

## Signature moves

1. **IMPORTANCE opens with clinical problem, closes with evidence gap.** The first sentence states the clinical problem; the second states what is unknown or lacking.
   - "Limited evidence is available regarding long-term outcomes of opioids compared with nonopioid medications for chronic pain." [Krebs 2018]
   - "Prediabetes is common, yet evidence-based lifestyle interventions are underutilized." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "Antidepressants for moderate to severe major depressive disorder may be discontinued prematurely because the prescribed antidepressant is not always the most appropriate medication for an individual. Guidelines have recommended more precise targeting of antidepressant treatment." [Cipriani 2026]
   - "Artificial intelligence (AI)-enabled scribes have been proposed to reduce electronic health record (EHR) burden and improve clinician satisfaction. There is limited evidence about their associated results across multiple sites and relative benefits for different clinician groups." [Rotenstein 2026]

2. **OBJECTIVE is a single sentence starting with "To [verb]."** The verb is clinical: "compare," "determine whether," "evaluate," "assess."
   - "To compare opioid vs nonopioid medications over 12 months on pain-related function, pain intensity, and adverse effects." [Krebs 2018]
   - "To determine whether referral to an exclusively artificial intelligence (AI)-led lifestyle intervention based on the Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) is noninferior to referral to a human-led DPP..." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "To evaluate the efficacy of a web-based tool to personalize antidepressant treatment." [Cipriani 2026]
   - "To assess the association of AI scribe adoption with changes in EHR time expenditure and visit volume and how associations vary by clinician characteristics." [Rotenstein 2026]

3. **RESULTS field states exact numbers with CI and P.** Format: "mean [outcome] was [X] in the [group] (SD, [Y]) vs [Z] in the [other group] (SD, [W]); difference, [D] (95% CI, [L] to [U])." or "adjusted relative risk, [X] (95% CI, [L] to [U]; P = [value])."
   - "mean 12-month BPI interference was 3.4 for the opioid group and 3.3 for the nonopioid group (difference, 0.1 [95% CI, -0.5 to 0.7])." [Krebs 2018]
   - "risk difference, -0.2% [1-sided 95% CI, -8.2%]), meeting the criterion for noninferiority." [Mathioudakis 2025]

4. **CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE restates the finding, then adds cautious clinical implication.** Uses "was not superior to," "was noninferior to," "was associated with." Never causal language for observational studies.
   - "Treatment with opioids was not superior to treatment with nonopioid medications for improving pain-related function over 12 months. Results do not support initiation of opioid therapy for moderate to severe chronic back pain or hip or knee osteoarthritis pain." [Krebs 2018]
   - "AI scribe adoption was associated with modest decreases in total EHR time and documentation time and with a modest increase in weekly visit volume." [Rotenstein 2026]

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT use an unstructured paragraph. JAMA requires labeled fields.
- Do NOT omit the Key Points box. It is a mandatory element for JAMA Original Investigations.
- Do NOT use causal language ("reduced," "improved," "caused") for observational studies. Use "was associated with."
- Do NOT include discussion or interpretation in the RESULTS field. State numbers only.
- Do NOT omit 95% CIs from the RESULTS field. Every key estimate needs a CI.
- Do NOT start OBJECTIVE with anything other than "To [verb]."
- Do NOT exceed ~350 words total across all fields.

## Paragraph structure

Not paragraph-based. Each labeled field is continuous inline text. The abstract reads as a single block with bold labels demarcating sections. No line breaks between fields in the published format (though the manuscript submission system may separate them).
