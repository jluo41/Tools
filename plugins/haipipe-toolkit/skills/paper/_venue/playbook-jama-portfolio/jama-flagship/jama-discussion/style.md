# JAMA Flagship Discussion -- Section Style Guide

Extracted from JAMA Original Investigation exemplars. Supplements `style-profile.md`.

## Word budget

- 800-1,500 words. JAMA Discussions are SHORT relative to Results and Methods. They interpret findings, compare to prior work, acknowledge limitations, and state conclusions.
- Krebs (2018): ~1,200w, Discussion + Limitations + Conclusions = 3 blocks. Mathioudakis (2025): ~1,400w. Cipriani (2026): ~1,500w. Rotenstein (2026): ~1,200w.
- Discussion has 2-3 dedicated subsections: the main body (untitled or titled "Discussion"), **Limitations**, and **Conclusions**. Limitations and Conclusions are separate subsections with their own red headings.

## Arc

```
restate key finding with design context (opening paragraph)
  -> comparison to prior work (2-3 paragraphs)
  -> mechanism / clinical interpretation (1-2 paragraphs)
  -> strengths (0-1 paragraph, sometimes folded into limitations)
  -> Limitations (dedicated subsection)
  -> Conclusions (dedicated subsection, 2-3 sentences)
```

## Signature moves

1. **Open by restating the main finding, naming the study design and population.** The first sentence of Discussion echoes the primary result but frames it in context. Pattern: "[Preposition] [population], [design finding] [did/did not] [result]."
   - "Among patients with chronic back pain or hip or knee osteoarthritis pain, treatment with opioids compared with nonopioid medications did not result in significantly better pain-related function over 12 months." [Krebs 2018]
   - "This RCT demonstrated that the percentage of participants achieving a composite outcome of weight loss, physical activity, and HbA1c reduction at 12 months following referral to an AI-driven DPP delivered without human intervention was noninferior to that of participants referred to a traditional human coach-based DPP." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "Compared with usual care, participants with MDD whose antidepressant was selected using the PETRUSHKA tool had a reduced risk by approximately 40% of discontinuing their antidepressant during the first 8 weeks of treatment." [Cipriani 2026]
   - "This study in 5 academic medical centers found that adoption of AI scribes was associated with modest reductions in total EHR time and documentation time." [Rotenstein 2026]

2. **Compare to prior literature using "consistent with" or "in contrast to."** 2-3 paragraphs position the finding against published evidence, citing specific studies.
   - "Recent systematic reviews have concluded that opioids have small beneficial effects on pain compared with placebo that may be outweighed by common adverse effects." [Krebs 2018]
   - "These multisite findings extend evidence from a growing body of single-site studies regarding the EHR time benefits of AI scribes..." [Rotenstein 2026]
   - "To our knowledge, the PETRUSHKA tool is the first coproduced, evidence-based, shared-decision support system to personalize treatment in depression. We systematically reviewed the available literature using study-level and individual-patient data from multiple sources..." [Cipriani 2026]

3. **Clinical interpretation offered cautiously, with hedging.** JAMA Discussion uses "may," "could," "it is possible that," "suggesting." No definitive causal claims from observational data.
   - "Nonopioid treatment was associated with significantly better pain intensity, but the clinical importance of this finding is unclear; the magnitude was small (0.5 points on the 0-10 BPI severity scale) and was less than the MCID of 1.0." [Krebs 2018]
   - "The AI-led DPP's advantage in facilitating higher engagement appeared to be offset by the human-led DPP's ability to confer benefit even among partial completers, resulting in comparable overall effectiveness." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "While AI scribes may not greatly reduce EHR time, given evidence regarding the burnout benefits of these technologies, it is possible that clinicians view reallocation of time from documentation as valuable, thus influencing improvements in clinician satisfaction." [Rotenstein 2026]

4. **Strengths listed before or within the Limitations subsection.** Often as a single paragraph: "This trial's pragmatic design has several advantages. First, ... Second, ... Third, ..."
   - "This trial's pragmatic design has several advantages. First, enrolled patients had characteristics similar to those of patients receiving opioids in VA primary care... Second, flexibility of treatment within assigned groups facilitated high study retention. Third, the treat-to-target approach reflects clinical practice more closely..." [Krebs 2018]
   - "Strengths of this trial include its randomized design, criterion-standard active control, 12-month duration, high retention, objective physical activity assessment, and pragmatic approach to DPP delivery." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "This study has both strengths and limitations. Strengths include the large sample size across multiple institutions, clinician types, and specialties." [Rotenstein 2026]

5. **Limitations as a dedicated subsection.** Opens with "This study has several limitations." Then enumerated: "First, ... Second, ... Third, ..." Each limitation names the threat and its direction of bias.
   - "This study has several limitations. First, the complexity of interventions precluded masking of patients. Because primary outcomes were patient-reported, results are subject to potential reporting bias that would likely favor opioids. Second, there was an imbalance in prerandomization treatment preference. ... Third, because this study was conducted in VA clinics, patient characteristics differ from those of the general population..." [Krebs 2018]
   - "This study has several limitations. First, it was not masked and used surrogate outcomes rather than incidence of diabetes. Second, despite CDC recognition... Third, while the AI algorithm for push notifications remained unchanged... Fourth, study visits and actigraphy were conducted outside of standard DPP exposures. ... Ninth, participants were motivated volunteers from only 2 sites, limiting generalizability..." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "This study has some limitations. First, we used an open design for patients and clinicians. ... Second, we used individual rather than cluster randomization. ... Third, the PETRUSHKA tool did not include information about all available antidepressants. ... Fourth, we focused only on acute treatment... Fifth, we recruited participants from 3 countries... Sixth, there was a lot of missing information..." [Cipriani 2026]
   - "These strengths are balanced by limitations. First, this was an observational study rather than a randomized clinical trial... Second, while clinician and time fixed effects were used in all models... Third, implementation details varied across the study sites... Fourth, the study's focus on academic institutions..." [Rotenstein 2026]

6. **Conclusions as a dedicated subsection, 2-3 sentences.** Restates the main finding and its clinical implication. Uses cautious language: "was not superior to," "was noninferior to," "was associated with." Final sentence points to future work.
   - "Treatment with opioids was not superior to treatment with nonopioid medications for improving pain-related function over 12 months. Results do not support initiation of opioid therapy for moderate to severe chronic back pain or hip or knee osteoarthritis pain." [Krebs 2018]
   - "Among adults with prediabetes and overweight or obesity, referral to a fully automated AI-led DPP was noninferior to referral to a human-led DPP in achieving a composite outcome based on weight reduction, physical activity, and HbA1c. Further studies are warranted to assess incidence of diabetes, generalizability to other AI-based programs, cost-effectiveness, implementation across diverse populations, and patient preference." [Mathioudakis 2025]
   - "This multisite, multivendor, controlled study found that AI scribe adoption was associated with modest but significant 3.0% relative decreases in total EHR time and 10.0% relative decreases in documentation time, as well as 1.7% relative increases in visit volume. ... Future studies should assess the longevity and reproducibility of these observations, as well as specific workflows and supports that can enhance the benefits of this technology." [Rotenstein 2026]

## Exemplar sentences (shape, not content)

**Discussion opener**:
- "Among [population], [intervention] compared with [comparator] [did not result in / was associated with] [outcome] over [time]." [general pattern]
- "This [study type] in [N] [settings] found that [intervention] was associated with [modest/significant] [change] in [outcome]." [general pattern]

**Comparison to prior work**:
- "Recent systematic reviews have concluded that [intervention] [has/shows] [finding] that [implication]." [general pattern]
- "These [multisite/large-scale] findings extend evidence from [prior work type]." [general pattern]

**Cautious interpretation**:
- "[Intervention] was associated with [outcome], but the clinical importance of this finding is unclear; the magnitude was [small/modest]." [general pattern]
- "It is possible that [mechanism], thus [influencing/contributing to] [outcome]." [general pattern]

**Limitations opener**:
- "This study has several limitations." [universal opener]

**Conclusions closer**:
- "[Finding restated]. Future studies should assess [next questions]." [general pattern]

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT omit the Limitations subsection. It is mandatory in JAMA and always titled "Limitations."
- Do NOT omit the Conclusions subsection. It is always a separate titled subsection in JAMA.
- Do NOT make the Discussion longer than Methods + Results combined. JAMA Discussions are concise.
- Do NOT introduce new data or analyses in Discussion. All data are in Results.
- Do NOT use definitive causal claims for observational data. "Was associated with," not "caused" or "led to."
- Do NOT skip the comparison to prior literature. JAMA expects at least 2-3 paragraphs of contextualization.
- Do NOT write a lengthy standalone Strengths subsection. Strengths are typically 1 paragraph, folded in before or within Limitations.
- Do NOT end the Conclusions with a method description. End with clinical or policy implication.

## Paragraph structure

| Block | Paragraphs | Job |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Key finding restatement | 1 | Restate primary result with design context |
| Comparison to prior work | 2-3 | Position against published evidence, cite specific studies |
| Clinical interpretation | 1-2 | Mechanism, clinical significance, policy implications |
| Strengths | 0-1 | Design advantages (optional, often brief) |
| **Limitations** (subsection) | 1-2 | "This study has several limitations. First, ... Second, ..." |
| **Conclusions** (subsection) | 1 | Restate finding + cautious implication + future directions |

Total discussion body: 5-8 paragraphs. Limitations and Conclusions are always the final two subsections.
