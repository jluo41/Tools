# Exemplar: Schroeder 2019 (JAMA Internal Medicine) - prescriber exposure -> opioid outcome

TARGET-OUTLET style exemplar: JAMA Internal Medicine, the outlet ProjB targets. A
prescriber-exposure -> downstream opioid outcome in a matched claims cohort. Mirror
this paper's voice most closely. Full PDF stored alongside this note
(`schroeder-2019-jamaim-dental-opioid.pdf`, author manuscript via Europe PMC PMC6439650).

## Citation

Schroeder AR, Dehghan M, Newman TB, Bentley JP, Park KT. Association of Opioid
Prescriptions From Dental Clinicians for US Adolescents and Young Adults With
Subsequent Opioid Use and Abuse. JAMA Intern Med. 2019;179(2):145-152.
doi:10.1001/jamainternmed.2018.5419. PMID 30508022.

## Title pattern to mirror

"Association of [exposure] From [source] for [population] With [outcome]." Plain,
associational, no causal verb. ProjB title can follow: "Association of Physician
Agreeableness With Opioid Prescribing Among ...".

## Structured-abstract shape (real section openers from the PDF)

- Importance: "Through prescription writing, dental clinicians are a potential source
  of initial opioid exposure and subsequent abuse for adolescents and young adults."
- Objective: "To examine the association between index dental opioid prescriptions ..."
- Design, Setting, and Participants: "This retrospective cohort study examined
  outpatient ..."
- Exposures / Main Outcomes and Measures / Results / Conclusions and Relevance: full
  JAMA IM structured set.
- Results phrasing to mirror: report the adjusted effect with absolute risk and CI,
  e.g. "(adjusted absolute risk difference, 6.8%; 95% CI, 6.3%-7.2%)". Effect sizes in
  clinical units, 95% CI, past tense, associational.
- Conclusions and Relevance: "The findings suggest that ..." (cautious, one to two
  sentences, the clinical takeaway).
