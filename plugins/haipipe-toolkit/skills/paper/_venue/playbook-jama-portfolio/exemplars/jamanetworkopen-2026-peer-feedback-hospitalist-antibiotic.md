# Exemplar: Witt 2026 (JAMA Network Open) - peer feedback and hospitalist prescribing

JAMA-family style exemplar (JAMA Network Open), Original Investigation. Physician
prescribing behavior with an "Association Between ..." title - the exact title pattern
and prescribing-behavior framing ProjB uses. Full text stored as
`jamanetworkopen-2026-peer-feedback-hospitalist-antibiotic.xml` (no render-PDF yet for
2026 articles; real full text in JATS XML).

## Citation

Witt LS, Prakash-Asrani R, Jones KA, et al. Association Between Peer Comparison
Feedback and Hospitalist Antibiotic Prescribing. JAMA Netw Open. 2026;9. e26.9504.
doi:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.9504. PMC13126217. Open access.

## Type and why it fits

- Article type: **Original Investigation** (full article; quality-improvement study).
- Title pattern: "Association Between [physician-level factor] and [prescribing
  behavior]" - the template ProjB's title can follow.
- Physician-level prescribing behavior in a health-care network.

## Style elements to mirror

- One-sentence study summary: "This quality improvement study assesses whether an
  intervention providing peer comparison feedback about orders for broad-spectrum
  antibiotics was associated with decreased antibiotic prescribing among hospitalists
  in a large health care network."
- Associational verb ("was associated with"), behavior outcome, network setting.
