# Exemplar: Burns 2024 (JAMA Network Open) - clinician contribution to opioid variation

JAMA-family style exemplar (same venue: JAMA Network Open). Physician-behavior topic:
how much of opioid administration variation is the CLINICIAN vs the hospital vs the
patient. Same "does the clinician matter, and how much" question shape as ProjB. Open
access, so the full text is fetchable for deeper imitation.

## Citation

Burns ML, Hilliard P, Vandervest J, et al. Variation in Intraoperative Opioid
Administration by Patient, Clinician, and Hospital Contribution. JAMA Netw Open.
2024;7(1):e2351689. doi:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2023.51689. Open access: PMC10792468.

## Why it is a good style exemplar

- Venue match: JAMA Network Open (JAMA family), not an adjacent journal.
- Physician-behavior framing: decomposes a prescribing/administration behavior into
  clinician / hospital / patient contributions.
- Cautious, associational, cohort-study voice that ProjB should mirror.

## Structured-abstract format to mirror (JAMA Network Open)

Section headers, in order: Importance / Objective / Design, Setting, and Participants /
Exposures / Main Outcomes and Measures / Results / Conclusions and Relevance.

- Conclusions and Relevance (verbatim, short): "In this cohort study, clinician-,
  hospital-, and patient-level factors had important contributions to substantial
  variation of opioid administrations during surgical procedures."

## Method phrasing to mirror

Clinician variation quantified with adjusted intraclass correlation coefficients
(ICCs) from generalized linear mixed models with random intercepts, patients nested
within clinicians within hospitals. ProjB can echo this "how much variation sits at
the clinician level" framing before attributing part of it to the trait.
