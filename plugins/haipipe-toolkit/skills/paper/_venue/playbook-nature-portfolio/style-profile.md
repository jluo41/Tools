# Nature Portfolio Style Profile (imitate this)

Distilled language style and preferences to imitate when writing or editing a Nature
Portfolio manuscript (`Nature`, `Nature Methods`, `Nature Biotechnology`). This is a
STARTER from Nature Portfolio conventions; ENRICH it from the actual papers stored in
`exemplars/` as they are added (pull real sentences and mirror their shapes).

## Abstract / summary paragraph

- Low-jargon opening that a non-specialist can follow; define field-specific terms
  before leaning on them.
- Arc: what is broadly at stake -> what was not possible or understood before -> the
  advance -> why it matters.
- End on significance, not the method or the dataset size.

## Significance / contribution statement

- One crisp statement of the broad advance, legible outside the immediate specialty.
- For flagship `Nature`: lead with conceptual significance for a wide readership.
- For `Nature Methods`: lead with enabling power (technical advance, validation,
  general utility, the biological application that proves it).
- For `Nature Biotechnology`: lead with biotechnology/translational significance, not
  bare technical novelty.

## Sentences

- Declarative, plain, broad-readership; avoid specialist density and acronym walls.
- One idea per sentence; minimal hype; no buzzword stacks.
- Make the "why it matters" explicit rather than implied.

## Article-type discipline

- Decide `Article` vs `Resource` vs `Analysis` (or short-format) early and write to it.
- A `Resource` reads around lasting reuse value; an `Article` reads around a linked
  evidence chain and a conceptual advance. Do not blur the two.

## Results & discussion

- Lead with the hero finding; benchmarking and validation follow.
- Restate the broad advance first in the discussion, then implications, then limits.
- Keep heavy methodological detail in the end-placed Methods and the supplement.

## Tone & preferences

- Measured, significance-centered, readable to non-specialists.
- Reporting: method executed by its own standards, with reproducibility, data/code
  availability, and protocol access ready (see the preflight in `README.md`).
- Treat image-integrity and AI-disclosure expectations as writing-time constraints.
