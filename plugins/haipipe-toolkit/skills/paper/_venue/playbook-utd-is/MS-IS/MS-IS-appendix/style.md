# MS-IS Online Appendix Style Guide

Sources: Huesmann et al. (2025, Management Science 72(2)), Cui et al. (2025,
Management Science Articles in Advance).

## Naming convention

Management Science calls its supplementary material the "Online Appendix" (not
"e-companion," "Electronic Companion," or "Supplementary Materials"). The front-page
metadata block reads:

> **Supplemental Material:** The online appendix and data files are available at
> https://doi.org/10.1287/mnsc.[manuscript-id].

Use singular "online appendix" when referring to a single document [huesmann-2025],
plural "online appendices" when multiple documents are bundled [cui-2025].

The legacy INFORMS prefix "EC." (Electronic Companion) survives in some element
numbering (e.g., "Proposition EC.1" [huesmann-2025]) but the document title itself
is "Online Appendix."

## Separate file, not inline

The Online Appendix is a separate PDF hosted at the DOI. It is not appended to the
main manuscript. A brief in-paper appendix (one figure or a short derivation) may
appear after the references in the main document [huesmann-2025: Figure A.1], but
all substantial supplementary content lives in the separate Online Appendix.

## Section and numbering scheme

Online Appendix sections are lettered: A, B, C, D, E, F. Subsections use dotted
notation within the letter: B.1, B.2, C.3, D.3 [huesmann-2025, cui-2025].

Two numbering conventions for floats appear in practice:

1. **Letter-prefixed** (more common): Tables and figures carry the appendix-section
   letter as a prefix. Table B.1, Table C.5, Figure A.1, Proposition EC.1
   [huesmann-2025].
2. **Continuous from main paper**: Figures continue the main-paper sequence (main
   paper ends at Figure 6; Online Appendix starts at Figure 7) [cui-2025].

Recommendation: use letter-prefixed numbering (Table A.1, Figure B.1) for clarity.
The continuous scheme risks confusion when the main paper is revised and figure
counts change.

## How the main text references appendix material

Standard phrasing patterns (draw from both papers):

- "see Online Appendix A" [huesmann-2025]
- "See Section B.2 of the Online Appendix" [huesmann-2025]
- "see Table B.1 in Online Appendix B" [huesmann-2025]
- "Table C.5 in Section C.3 of the Online Appendix" [huesmann-2025]
- "See Figure 9 in the Online Appendix" [cui-2025]
- "results from this experiment are ... relegated to Online Appendix E" [cui-2025]
- "in Online Appendix D" [cui-2025]

Always name the specific section letter or table/figure number. Never write a bare
"see the Online Appendix" without a section or item reference.

## What goes in the appendix vs. main text (triage rule)

**Main text** (13-16 printed pages):
- Core theory, hypotheses, and the primary identification strategy.
- Experiment or research design (enough for the reader to evaluate).
- Main results (primary outcome, 1-2 key tables/figures).
- Heterogeneity analysis when it is a primary contribution.
- Discussion, limitations, conclusion.

**Online Appendix** (typically longer than the main text):
- Formal model derivations, proofs, and propositions [huesmann-2025: Appendix A].
- Full experiment instructions and parameter tables [huesmann-2025: Appendix B].
- Robustness and sensitivity checks (alternative specifications, Tobit, Poisson,
  unweighted estimates) [huesmann-2025: Appendix C; cui-2025: Appendix F].
- Supplementary experiments or data from abandoned/secondary experiments
  [cui-2025: Appendix E].
- Intent-to-treat (ITT) results alongside LATE in main [cui-2025: Appendix B].
- Extended heterogeneity (quartile splits, alternative cutoffs) [cui-2025: Appendix C].
- Cross-site or cross-experiment comparisons [cui-2025: Appendix D].
- Additional figures that support but are not essential to the main argument.
- Data construction details, variable definitions, balance tables.

**Rule of thumb:** if removing the item would prevent the reader from evaluating the
paper's primary claim, it belongs in the main text. If it strengthens or extends the
claim but is not required to follow the argument, it belongs in the Online Appendix.

## Length norms

Main papers run 13-16 typeset pages [huesmann-2025: 16 pp; cui-2025: 13 pp]. There
is no stated page limit for the Online Appendix, and it routinely exceeds the main
paper in length. A paper with 3-6 lettered appendix sections and 10-20 supplementary
tables/figures is typical for empirical MS-IS work.

## In-paper appendix (optional, brief)

A short in-paper appendix may appear after References, before the Online Appendix
link. It is reserved for a single illustrative example or a key derivation that the
reader needs without leaving the document [huesmann-2025: one figure, "Appendix.
Illustrative Example"]. It is not a substitute for the Online Appendix.

## Endnotes convention

Both papers use numbered endnotes (superscript in text, listed after the main body)
for clarifications that do not warrant Online Appendix treatment. Endnotes
frequently cross-reference Online Appendix sections: "For more information, see
Section C of the Online Appendix" [huesmann-2025, endnote 4].
