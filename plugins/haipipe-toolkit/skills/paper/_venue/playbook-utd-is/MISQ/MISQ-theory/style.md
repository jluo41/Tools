# MISQ Theory / Hypotheses -- Section Style Guide

Extracted from MISQ Research Article exemplars. Supplements `style-profile.md`.

This is MISQ's signature section. It does the most distinctive work and is the section reviewers scrutinize hardest. The section is called "Hypotheses," "Literature Review and Hypotheses," "Theoretical Framework," or "Background and Prior Literature" + "Hypotheses" -- but regardless of title, it performs the same job: derive testable predictions from named theoretical mechanisms.

## Word budget

- 3,000-6,000 words (6-12 published pages two-column). This is the longest section in a typical MISQ Research Article.
- Liu (2021): ~4,500w across "Hypotheses" with subsections per construct (pp. 1117-1121, 5 pages).
- Gao (2015): ~5,500w across "Background and Prior Literature" + "Hypotheses" (pp. 2-12, pre-typeset).
- Yin (2014): ~4,000w across "Literature Review and Hypotheses" (pp. 540-544, 5 pages).

## Arc

```
[optional: lit review / background subsection(s)]
  -> theoretical framework introduction (name the theory/theories)
  -> mechanism identification (name 2-4 mechanisms or streams)
  -> per-construct / per-hypothesis derivation subsections:
       define construct -> cite evidence mechanism exists
       -> argue why it applies HERE -> state directional consequence
       -> H_n: [construct] is [positively/negatively] related to [outcome].
  -> [optional: competing hypotheses where theory is ambiguous]
```

## Signature moves

1. **Hypotheses embedded at end of each subsection, not collected separately.** Each H closes the argument that derived it. There is NO standalone "Hypotheses" subsection that lists H1-H5 together. Each subsection earns its hypothesis through derivation, then states it at the end.
   - Liu (2021): H1 at end of "Openness" subsection, H2 at end of "Conscientiousness," etc.
   - Gao (2015): H1a/H1b at end of "Behavioral and Environmental Influences" subsection, H2 at end of "Hyperbole Effects."
   - Yin (2014): H1 and H2 stated together at end of the theory development, after the derivation chain is complete.

2. **Named mechanism streams.** The theory section identifies 2-4 named theoretical mechanisms through which the IV affects the DV, then routes each construct through those streams. This creates a structured, reusable derivation pattern rather than ad hoc reasoning.
   - "We hypothesize relationships between reviewer personality and review helpfulness through three mechanisms: (1) knowledge sharing, (2) reviewer persuasiveness, and (3) opinion leadership." [Liu 2021]
   - Gao (2015) uses two mechanism categories: (1) self-selection (customer complaining behavior) and (2) social influence (conformity/anchoring).

3. **Competing hypotheses when theory is ambiguous.** When the theoretical direction is genuinely uncertain, MISQ papers state competing predictions (H1a vs H1b) rather than forcing a single direction. The paper then says "our objective is not to isolate a specific mechanism ... but rather to provide empirical insight into the overall relationship." [Gao 2015]
   - "H1a: Physicians of lower quality are more likely to be rated online than the average and high-quality physicians."
   - "H1b: Physicians of lower quality are less likely to be rated online than the average and high-quality physicians." [Gao 2015]

4. **Define-cite-apply-predict per paragraph.** Each theoretical paragraph follows a four-step micro-structure: (a) define the construct or mechanism, (b) cite evidence it exists in prior literature, (c) argue why it applies in THIS specific context, (d) state the directional consequence. This is the mechanism-derivation discipline that distinguishes theory from lit review.
   - Example from Liu (2021) Openness subsection: defines openness (McCrae and Costa 2003) -> cites link to knowledge sharing (Cabrera et al. 2006) -> argues open individuals contribute more helpful knowledge in review settings -> predicts H1.

5. **One construct per subsection, one hypothesis per construct.** MISQ avoids long undifferentiated H1-H12 lists. Each subsection develops one construct at proper length (3-6 paragraphs), then derives one focused hypothesis. When a construct has complex effects (e.g., emotional stability through both persuasiveness and opinion leadership channels), the competing forces are developed within the subsection and resolved into a single H.

6. **Citation density: ~0.50 citations per sentence.** About half of all sentences carry a citation; the other half are inferential/reasoning bridges. Citations are mandatory for construct definitions and established-relationship claims. Citations are absent from logical inference steps and hypothesis statements themselves.

## Exemplar sentences (shape, not content)

**Mechanism introduction**:
- "We hypothesize relationships between reviewer personality and review helpfulness through three mechanisms: (1) knowledge sharing, (2) reviewer persuasiveness, and (3) opinion leadership." [Liu 2021]
- "The multiple factors that may distort the relationship between online ratings and quality broadly fall into two categories: self-selection and social influence." [Gao 2015]
- "Our primary argument is that the emotions embedded in a review affect reader perceptions of the cognitive effort expended by the reviewer, which in turn affect perceptions of review helpfulness." [Yin 2014]

**Construct definition (opening of a derivation subsection)**:
- "Openness, also known as openness to experience, is characterized by active imagination, aesthetic sensitivity, attentiveness to inner feelings, preference for variety, and intellectual curiosity (McCrae and Costa 2003)." [Liu 2021]
- "Agreeableness is associated with characteristics such as cooperation, likeability, forgivingness, kindness, sympathy, and trust (McCrae and Costa 2003)." [Liu 2021]

**Mechanism application (argue why it applies here)**:
- "Therefore, those individuals are likely to develop more expertise that enables them to give helpful advice and share their knowledge (Constant et al. 1996). Based on this argument, we expect persons high in openness will be more engaged in seeking and contributing knowledge and, in our setting, those persons would be expected to write more helpful reviews." [Liu 2021]
- "Applied to the current setting, therefore, review writers experiencing anxiety can be expected to devote more cognitive effort to the review task." [Yin 2014]

**Hypothesis statement**:
- "H1: Reviewer openness is positively related to review helpfulness." [Liu 2021]
- "H4: Reviewer agreeableness is positively related to review helpfulness." [Liu 2021]
- "H1a: Physicians of lower quality are more likely to be rated online than the average and high-quality physicians." [Gao 2015]
- "Hypothesis 1: Anxiety-embedded reviews are perceived to be more helpful than anger-embedded reviews." [Yin 2014]

**Competing-prediction transition**:
- "Up to this point, neither literature nor theory has determined which of these hypotheses may be dominant. Our objective is not to isolate a specific mechanism (as there are many), but rather to provide empirical insight into the overall relationship between physician quality and physician ratings." [Gao 2015]

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT list all hypotheses in a standalone "Hypotheses" section separate from their derivation. Each H must close the argument that derived it.
- Do NOT assert hypotheses without named theoretical mechanisms. "We expect X because it seems reasonable" is not theory.
- Do NOT confuse lit review with theory. A paragraph that says "Smith et al. found X; Jones et al. found Y; Lee et al. found Z" is lit review. A theory paragraph says "Because [mechanism], we expect [consequence] in this context."
- Do NOT include measurement or operationalization details in the theory section. How the construct is measured belongs in Methods. The theory takes the construct as given and derives what it should predict.
- Do NOT produce an H1-H12 fishing expedition. 3-5 focused hypotheses, each earning a full subsection of development, is the MISQ norm.
- Do NOT make tautological hypotheses ("users who find a system useful will use it"). The hypothesis must name a non-obvious mechanism.

## Paragraph structure

### Overall section structure (6-12 pages)

| Subsection | Paragraphs | Job |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Background / Lit positioning | 2-4 | Survey what is known, identify where prior work stops |
| Theoretical framework intro | 1-2 | Name the theory or mechanism streams, explain the overall logic |
| Per-construct subsection (repeated 3-5x) | 3-6 each | Define construct -> route through mechanisms -> derive prediction -> state H |

### Per-construct subsection internal structure (3-6 paragraphs)

| Paragraph | Job |
|----------|-----|
| P1 | Define the construct, cite canonical sources |
| P2 | Route through mechanism 1 (e.g., knowledge sharing): evidence + application to context |
| P3 | Route through mechanism 2 (e.g., persuasiveness): evidence + application to context |
| P4 | Route through mechanism 3 (e.g., opinion leadership): evidence + application to context |
| P5 | Synthesize: "Given that [construct] is positively related to all three mechanisms, we hypothesize:" |
| H | Bolded or italicized hypothesis statement |

Not every construct requires routing through every mechanism stream. Some subsections are 3 paragraphs (define -> single mechanism -> H). The pattern is consistent but the depth varies by theoretical richness.

## Enriched from additional exemplars (2026-06-29)

Source papers: Zhang (2025), Weng (2026), Ayabakan (2025), Raimi (2025), Liu-EBM (2025), Liu-HMM (2025).

### Section naming variants (expanded)

The theory section now appears under a wider set of titles:
- "Hypothesis Development" [Zhang 2025]
- "Model Development" [Weng 2026]
- "Prior Research and Theory" [Raimi 2025]
- "Conceptual Development" [Ayabakan 2025]
- "SR Automation: Task Complexity, Design Requirements, and Related Literature" [Liu-EBM 2025]
- "Research Background" [Liu-HMM 2025]

### Additional signature moves

7. **Temporal or policy-structure hypotheses.** Healthcare and platform papers increasingly derive hypotheses about the TEMPORAL dimension of an effect (during vs. after a policy), not just cross-sectional relationships. The hypothesis itself specifies a time-indexed prediction.
   - "H1: Introductory incentives have a positive impact on physician contributions during the policy window." [Zhang 2025]
   - "H2: Introductory incentives have a negative impact on physician contributions after the policy window ends." [Zhang 2025]

8. **Moderation hypotheses using higher-order constructs.** Personality and behavioral papers derive moderation hypotheses through meta-trait aggregation (e.g., Big Five to stability/plasticity) rather than testing all 5 traits individually. This compresses the hypothesis space.
   - Weng (2026) aggregates conscientiousness + agreeableness + emotional stability into "team stability" and extraversion + openness into "team plasticity," then derives two focused moderation hypotheses rather than ten.

9. **Reference-point / mental-accounting derivation.** Papers applying behavioral economics theories to IS contexts derive hypotheses through the lens of reference points, anchoring, or loss aversion. The derivation pattern names the reference point, argues why it applies here, and predicts the directional consequence.
   - "According to mental accounting theory (Thaler, 1999), if the online incentives are processed in a segregated account, individuals will develop a reference point in this account and evaluate the incentives in this account independently" [Zhang 2025]
   - Zhang (2025) derives four interaction hypotheses (H3a/H3b/H4a/H4b) exploring online vs. offline income as competing reference points.

10. **Research question format instead of formal hypotheses.** Research Notes and health IT papers sometimes use numbered research questions (RQ1, RQ2) instead of formal hypotheses, especially when the contribution is empirical rather than theoretical.
    - "RQ1: Does EHR adoption lead to a decrease in claim denials?" [Ayabakan 2025]
    - Ayabakan (2025) uses "Conceptual Development" rather than "Hypotheses Development" and does not state formal H1/H2.

11. **Theory section in design science papers: Design Requirements (DR) format.** For computational design contributions, the theory section replaces hypotheses with numbered Design Requirements (DR1-DR4), each derived from a task complexity analysis and grounded in prior literature.
    - "DR1: The proposed ML automation solution for data extraction should require minimal labeling effort." [Liu-EBM 2025]
    - Each DR follows the same four-step pattern: Task complexity -> Related literature -> Gap -> DR statement.

12. **PRISMA-style systematic lit review within theory section.** Raimi (2025) conducts a systematic literature review (using PRISMA guidelines) WITHIN the theory section to establish that no prior work meets their two criteria, providing unusually rigorous evidence for the gap. This is a new move for MISQ.

13. **Multi-study grounded theory loop.** Raimi (2025) uses qualitative grounded theory (Study 2) to DISCOVER constructs between two quantitative studies (Study 1 and Study 3). The theory section is built iteratively across studies rather than all upfront. This is acceptable in mixed-methods MISQ papers.

### Healthcare-domain personality-trait conventions

- When using the Big Five in healthcare/IS contexts, MISQ papers cite Costa and McCrae (1992) or McCrae and Costa (2003) as the canonical source for personality definitions.
- Weng (2026) demonstrates that the meta-trait level (stability/plasticity from DeYoung et al., 2002; Digman, 1997) is acceptable for team-level analysis, compressing the dimensionality.
- Construct definitions for personality traits follow the canonical format: "[Trait] is [definition from canonical source] (cite). [Trait] is associated with [behavioral characteristics] in [prior context] (cite)."
- When personality is measured from text (as in Liu 2021), a validation subsection is expected. When measured by self-report survey (as in Weng 2026), standard psychometric reporting (Cronbach's alpha, AVE, factor loadings) suffices.

### Updated anti-patterns

- Do NOT mix DR-format and H-format in the same paper. Choose one based on the contribution type: hypotheses for behavioral/empirical papers, DRs for design science papers.
- Do NOT derive hypotheses about personality without citing the canonical Big Five source (Costa & McCrae 1992 or McCrae & Costa 2003).
- Do NOT derive 10+ hypotheses. Even complex moderation papers (Zhang 2025 with 7 hypotheses; Weng 2026 with 2 hypotheses) stay disciplined. More hypotheses = more subsections, and each must earn its derivation.

### Exemplar sentences from new papers

**Mechanism introduction**:
- "We mainly draw on mental accounting theory, which suggests that people create a set of mental accounts to organize, evaluate, and keep track of financial activities" [Zhang 2025]
- "We postulate that the effectiveness of process controls in alleviating technical risks is contingent on project team personality." [Weng 2026]

**Construct definition (personality-specific)**:
- "Team stability is defined as a personality meta-trait reflecting a group of individuals' tendency to maintain the status quo and avoid disruption. This meta-trait relates to socially desirable personality traits -- namely, conscientiousness, agreeableness, and emotional stability (opposite of neuroticism) (Digman, 1997)." [Weng 2026]
- "Team plasticity relates to the team's tendency to engage in flexible and novel courses of action (DeYoung et al., 2002)." [Weng 2026]

**Hypothesis statement (temporal)**:
- "H1: Introductory incentives have a positive impact on physician contributions during the policy window." [Zhang 2025]
- "H3a: Online income strengthens the positive effect of introductory incentives on physician contributions during the policy window." [Zhang 2025]

**Hypothesis statement (moderation)**:
- "H1: A team with a high level of stability will respond more positively to internal process control than a team with a low level of stability, increasing the mitigating effects of internal process control on the negative effects of technical IT project risks on project outcomes." [Weng 2026]

**Design requirement statement**:
- "DR1: The proposed ML automation solution for data extraction should require minimal labeling effort." [Liu-EBM 2025]
