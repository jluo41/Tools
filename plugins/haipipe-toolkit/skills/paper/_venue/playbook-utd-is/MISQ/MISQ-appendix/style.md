# MISQ Appendix Style Guide

Patterns extracted from two published MISQ research articles.

Sources:
- [zhang-2025] Zhang et al. (2025). "Engaging Physicians with Introductory Incentives." MISQ 49(3), pp. 917-952.
- [weng-2026] Weng et al. (2026). "Extending the IT Risk Control Framework." MISQ 50(1), pp. 87-114.

## Naming Convention

The appendix is titled simply "Appendix" followed by a capital letter: Appendix A, Appendix B, Appendix C, etc. [zhang-2025, weng-2026]. There is no "Online Appendix" or "Online Supplement" label. The appendix is part of the same published PDF, not a separate supplementary file. Each appendix section gets a bold descriptive subtitle beneath the letter heading (e.g., "Appendix A: Measuring Offline and Online Income" [zhang-2025], "Appendix A: Measurement" [weng-2026]).

## Numbering and Lettering Scheme

Tables and figures in the appendix use the appendix letter as a prefix, followed by a sequential number within that appendix:

- Tables: Table A1, Table A2, Table B1, Table C1, Table E1, Table F1, Table G1, Table G2, Table H1, Table H2, Table I1 [zhang-2025]. Table A1, Table B1, Table B2 [weng-2026].
- Figures: No appendix figures were used in either paper.

The numbering restarts at 1 within each appendix letter. The pattern is always "Table [Letter][Number]" with no separator (not "Table A-1" or "Table A.1").

## What Goes in the Appendix vs. Main Text

Triage rule: the main text carries the core argument (hypotheses, primary models, primary results). The appendix carries everything a skeptical reviewer needs to verify those results without cluttering the narrative.

Content that belongs in the appendix:
- Variable construction details and measurement items (how a variable was built from raw data) [zhang-2025 Appendix A; weng-2026 Appendix A]
- Matching and sample construction diagnostics (balance tables, covariate comparisons) [zhang-2025 Appendix B]
- Supplementary regression results referenced by the main text (e.g., month-by-month breakdowns of a main effect) [zhang-2025 Appendix C]
- Alternative dependent variable analyses [zhang-2025 Appendix D]
- Alternative model specifications as robustness checks [zhang-2025 Appendix E]
- Alternative variable constructions as robustness checks [zhang-2025 Appendix F]
- Alternative time windows or sample periods [zhang-2025 Appendix G]
- Alternative matching strategies (CEM without k2k, PSM) [zhang-2025 Appendix H]
- Alternative control groups [zhang-2025 Appendix I]
- Full measurement scales and survey items [weng-2026 Appendix A]
- Descriptive statistics, correlation matrices, endogeneity tests [weng-2026 Appendix B]

Content that belongs in the main text:
- Primary model results (the tables that directly test hypotheses)
- Alternative explanation analyses (intrinsic motivation, contribution quality, patient selection, patient herding) that constitute a substantive section of the argument [zhang-2025 kept these in the main text]
- Research model figures and interaction plots

## How the Main Text References Appendix Material

References are parenthetical and concise, placed at the end of the relevant sentence or clause:

- "see Appendix A for more details" [zhang-2025]
- "We discuss the comparability between the two groups in more detail in Appendix B" [zhang-2025]
- "We report more details of these results in Appendix C" [zhang-2025]
- "Appendix Table E1 presents the results" [zhang-2025]
- "see Appendix A for more details" [weng-2026]
- "in Appendix B" [weng-2026]

When referencing a specific table: "Appendix Table E1" or "Table E1 in Appendix E" (the word "Appendix" is capitalized). The main text never reproduces appendix content; it states the finding in one sentence and points the reader to the appendix for full results.

## Typical Content Structure per Appendix

Each appendix section follows a brief pattern:

1. A bold heading: "Appendix [Letter]" (large font, section-level heading).
2. A descriptive subtitle (bold, subsection-level).
3. One to three paragraphs of explanatory prose describing the analysis or construction.
4. One or more tables presenting the results.

Prose is minimal. A short appendix (e.g., alternative DV or alternative matching) may contain only 2-4 sentences of explanation followed by a single table [zhang-2025 Appendices D, I]. A longer appendix (e.g., measurement items) may span 2-3 pages of tables [weng-2026 Appendix A].

## Length Norms

- [zhang-2025]: 9 appendix sections (A through I), spanning 12 pages (pp. 940-951) out of a 36-page article (33% of total length). Main text is 20 pages (pp. 917-936), references 3 pages, author bios 1 page.
- [weng-2026]: 2 appendix sections (A and B), spanning 4 pages (pp. 111-114) out of a 28-page article (14% of total length). Main text is 19 pages (pp. 87-105), references 4 pages, author bios 2 pages.

Appendix length varies widely by paper type. Empirical papers with causal identification (DID, matching) tend toward longer appendices because robustness checks multiply. Survey-based papers tend toward shorter appendices focused on measurement instruments. An appendix consuming 15-35% of total article length is within the observed range.

## Document Structure

The appendix is part of the same PDF, published within the same article. It appears after the References and About the Authors sections, as the final content before the copyright notice. There is no separate supplementary file.

## Summary Checklist

- Label: "Appendix [A-Z]" with descriptive subtitle
- Table numbering: Table [Letter][Number], no separator (Table A1, not Table A-1)
- Minimum prose, maximum tables
- Main text references via "see Appendix X" or "Appendix Table X1"
- Robustness checks are the primary appendix payload for empirical papers
- Measurement items and variable construction details belong in the appendix
- Same document, after References and author bios
- No page limit observed, but keep each appendix section self-contained
