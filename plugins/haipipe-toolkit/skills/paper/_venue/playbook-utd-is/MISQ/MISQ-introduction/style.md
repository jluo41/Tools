# MISQ Introduction -- Section Style Guide

Extracted from MISQ Research Article exemplars. Supplements `style-profile.md`.

## Word budget

- 2,000-4,000 words (5-8 published pages, two-column). The introduction in MISQ is notably LONG compared to economics or clinical journals. It often absorbs what other venues split into "Introduction" + "Related Work" + "Research Overview."
- Liu (2021): ~3,500w across "Introduction" + "Research Context and Motivations" + "Contributions" + "Research Design" (pp. 1113-1116, 4 pages).
- Gao (2015): ~2,000w across "Introduction" only (pp. 1-2, pre-typeset).
- Yin (2014): ~2,500w across "Introduction" (pp. 539-540, 2 pages two-column).

## Arc

```
phenomenon hook (IS context, why it matters)
  -> what is known (brief lit positioning, 1-2 paragraphs)
  -> gap / what is not known (specific, named)
  -> our approach (what we do + what is new + how we do it)
  -> key findings (preview, 1-2 sentences)
  -> contributions (numbered or prose)
  -> roadmap sentence (optional, sometimes absorbed)
```

## Signature moves

1. **Open with a phenomenon hook, not a literature survey.** The first sentence grounds the paper in a real-world IS phenomenon that matters to both researchers and practitioners. It is NOT "Prior research has studied X." It IS "[Something important] has happened / is happening."
   - Source: all three exemplars.

2. **The "what we do / what is new / how we do it" triple.** MISQ introductions frequently contain an explicit sentence or paragraph that announces these three things, sometimes literally using those words.
   - "Below, we introduce what we do, what is new, and how we do it." [Liu 2021 -- literal]
   - Gao (2015) delivers the same triple but without the meta-announcement.

3. **Contribution declaration with numbered list or prose block.** Unlike some venues that bury contributions in discussion, MISQ introductions contain a visible contribution declaration within the introduction itself, often as "The research presented here makes several important contributions. First, ... Second, ... Third, ..." [Gao 2015] or as a dedicated subsection "Contributions to the Product Review Literature and Actionable Implications" [Liu 2021].

4. **First-study claim (used sparingly).** "First, to the best of our knowledge, this study is the first to provide empirical evidence of the relationship between [X] and [Y]." [Gao 2015]. This move is acceptable in MISQ but must be precise and defensible.

5. **Figure 1 as positioning device.** Liu (2021) includes "Figure 1. Comparison of this Study and Prior Literature" within the introduction -- a visual showing how this paper differs from prior work. This is a MISQ signature for papers that need to carve space against a crowded literature.

6. **Subsectioned introduction.** MISQ Research Articles often break the introduction into titled subsections rather than running it as one undifferentiated block. Liu (2021): "Introduction" / "Research Context and Motivations" / "Contributions to the Product Review Literature" / "Research Design". This is acceptable and common.

## Exemplar sentences (shape, not content)

**Phenomenon hook** (first sentence of paper):
- "Recent years have witnessed an explosion of machine learning (e.g., neural network), aiding many advances in science and technology." [Liu 2021]
- "Recent years have witnessed a rapid growth of online ratings by consumers across many products and services." [Gao 2015]
- "Online reviews have played an increasingly important role in the popularity and success of electronic commerce." [Yin 2014]

**Gap statement**:
- "Surprisingly, in spite of the pervasiveness of this assumption, little empirical evidence exists demonstrating the relationship between online ratings and product quality." [Gao 2015]
- "Although past work has shown that emotions can substantially influence the way that reviews are processed, prior research has not adequately addressed the impact of distinct emotions on review helpfulness, nor has it explained those effects." [Yin 2014]

**Approach/what-is-new**:
- "We propose to inform review platforms of unacquainted reviewers' personalities as an antecedent, or predictor, of review helpfulness." [Liu 2021]
- "In this paper we provide insights into the relationship between online ratings and the underlying consumer population's perception of quality." [Gao 2015]
- "Our research focuses on the connection between the emotional content of a review and its perceived helpfulness." [Yin 2014]

**Contribution declaration**:
- "The research presented here makes several important contributions. First, to the best of our knowledge, this study is the first to provide empirical evidence of the relationship between online ratings and population perceptions of quality. Second, we extend research on online word-of-mouth into the domain of professional services." [Gao 2015]
- "Our work adds to a growing body of information systems research highlighting the role of emotions." [Yin 2014]

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT start the paper with a literature review paragraph ("Smith et al. (2020) found that..."). Start with the phenomenon.
- Do NOT leave contributions implicit. State them explicitly, either in a dedicated subsection or a numbered block.
- Do NOT end the introduction without previewing findings. The reader should know the punchline before entering the theory section.
- Do NOT write a one-paragraph introduction. MISQ introductions are multi-paragraph, often multi-subsection.
- Do NOT omit the gap. Even when the contribution is methodological, there must be a named conceptual or empirical gap.

## Paragraph structure

MISQ introductions typically contain 7-12 paragraphs organized into blocks:

| Block | Paragraphs | Job |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Hook | 1-2 | Phenomenon, why it matters, scale/impact |
| Known | 1-2 | What prior work established, brief positioning |
| Gap | 1 | What is NOT known, why that matters |
| Approach | 1-2 | What we do, data/method overview, research design |
| Findings preview | 1 | Key results, directional |
| Contributions | 1-2 | Explicit contribution claims, numbered or prose |
| Roadmap | 0-1 | "The remainder of this paper is organized as follows..." (optional) |

The introduction may be one flat section or broken into 2-4 titled subsections. Both formats appear in published MISQ papers.

## Enriched from additional exemplars (2026-06-29)

Source papers: Zhang (2025), Weng (2026), Ayabakan (2025), Raimi (2025), Liu-EBM (2025), Liu-HMM (2025).

### Updated word budget

- Zhang (2025): ~3,200w across Introduction + Related Literature (~4 pages). Follows the long-introduction pattern with lit review folded in.
- Weng (2026): ~2,800w across Introduction only (~3 pages). Self-contained, no separate lit section in intro.
- Ayabakan (2025, Research Note): ~1,500w across Introduction (~2 pages). Research Notes compress the introduction significantly.
- Raimi (2025): ~2,000w across Introduction (~2 pages). Multi-study papers front-load the study sequence in the introduction.
- Liu-EBM (2025): ~3,000w across Introduction (~3 pages). Design science papers devote extra introduction space to the problem/artifact framing.
- Liu-HMM (2025, Research Note): ~1,800w across Introduction (~2 pages).

### Additional signature moves

7. **Dollar-value or impact-statistic hook for healthcare papers.** Healthcare-domain MISQ papers open with a concrete dollar figure or prevalence statistic to establish the real-world stakes before any literature positioning. This is more aggressive than the general "phenomenon hook" and is specific to health IT and policy papers.
   - "In the U.S. healthcare industry, approximately $745 billion of care was provided without compensation in 2000-2020" [Ayabakan 2025]
   - "During COVID-19, there was a surge in the OHC user base, which ballooned to 8-10 times larger than before on a daily basis" [Zhang 2025]

8. **Explicit research questions (RQ1, RQ2) in introduction.** Several new papers state numbered research questions within the introduction, not just a general "we examine" statement. This is especially common in healthcare IS and Research Notes.
   - "RQ1: Does EHR adoption lead to a decrease in claim denials? RQ2: Does the extent to which a hospital adopts EHR applications from the same EHR vendor ... lead to a decrease in claim denials?" [Ayabakan 2025]
   - "How do introductory incentives influence the contributions of physicians new to an OHC during and after the introductory policy window?" [Zhang 2025]

9. **2x2 positioning table in the introduction or related work.** Multiple papers use a positioning table (not just a figure) in the introduction to show where the current paper fills a gap in the literature matrix. This extends the Figure 1 positioning device noted above.
   - Zhang (2025) includes "Table 1. Related Literature on Monetary Incentives in Online Communities" as a 2x2 matrix (launch/decrease x voluntary/for-reward) showing "This paper" in the underexplored quadrant.

10. **Multi-study roadmap in introduction.** Multi-study papers preview the study sequence in the introduction with explicit labels (Study 1, Study 2, Study 3).
    - "We began with two experiments (Study 1 comprised a main study and a robustness check) ... we took a step back and conducted a qualitative interview study (Study 2) ... We conducted two additional experiments (Study 3)" [Raimi 2025]

11. **Theory-as-lens declaration.** Recent papers explicitly name the theory as a "lens" or "perspective" being applied, making the theoretical commitment more visible in the introduction.
    - "We mainly draw on mental accounting theory" [Zhang 2025]
    - "Grounded in the 'agentic' systems delegation framework and instance-based learning theory" [Liu-HMM 2025]

12. **"Cautionary" or "counterintuitive" framing for contribution.** When findings are unexpected, the introduction previews this with cautionary language rather than straightforward contribution claims.
    - "Our findings provide a cautionary perspective on the unintended consequences of using introductory incentives" [Zhang 2025]
    - "Our study shows that perceptions of judgmentalness are actually the opposite of what has been assumed" [Raimi 2025]

### Research Note vs. Research Article introduction differences

Research Notes (Ayabakan 2025, Liu-HMM 2025) follow the same arc but are compressed:
- 1,500-1,800w vs. 2,500-3,500w for Research Articles.
- Typically no subsectioned introduction; one continuous block.
- Contributions are stated in 1-2 sentences, not a numbered list.
- The method/approach description is shorter (1 paragraph vs. 2-3).
- Findings preview is brief (1-2 sentences).

### Updated anti-patterns

- Do NOT open with a literature review paragraph. This is strongly confirmed: all 6 new papers open with a phenomenon or impact hook.
- Do NOT write a Research Note introduction at Research Article length or vice versa. The 1,500w vs. 3,000w difference is systematic.

### Exemplar sentences from new papers

**Phenomenon hook (first sentence)**:
- "Online communities are internet-based platforms that allow different parties to exchange information and resources (Gu et al., 2007)." [Zhang 2025]
- "With information technology (IT) playing a critical role in the economy, organizational change has become heavily driven by IT project implementations" [Weng 2026]
- "In the U.S. healthcare industry, approximately $745 billion of care was provided without compensation in 2000-2020" [Ayabakan 2025]
- "The mental health crisis has seen a dramatic increase over the last few decades and is considered a silent epidemic of the 21st century" [Raimi 2025]
- "Artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping traditional business process management" [Liu-HMM 2025]

**Gap statement**:
- "However, the literature has yet to investigate why certain IT project teams respond better than others to process controls." [Weng 2026]
- "to the best of our knowledge, the HIT literature has not extensively examined the implications of EHR sourcing decisions within and across hospitals" [Ayabakan 2025]
- "no prior empirical research has assessed the judgmentalness displayed by chatbots compared to the judgmentalness displayed by professional healthcare providers" [Raimi 2025]

**Contribution declaration**:
- "Our study makes important contributions to the literature. We first advance the understanding of the dual impacts ... Second, ... we explain the change in user behavior from a unique mental accounting perspective. Third, ..." [Zhang 2025]
- "This study expands the body of IS knowledge on HIT by exploring another critical role of EHRs in tackling the pressing challenges in the U.S. healthcare system" [Ayabakan 2025]
