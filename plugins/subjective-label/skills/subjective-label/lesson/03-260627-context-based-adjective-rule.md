Single generic adjectives ("great", "excellent", "terrible") cannot determine subjective dimensions like agreeableness. The same word maps to clinical competence or interpersonal manner depending on context. "Great surgeon, fixed my knee" = no interpersonal evidence. "Great listener, made me feel heard" = interpersonal evidence. Guidelines must require direct behavioral evidence, not keyword matching. This is a tiebreaker rule that emerged in the first shallow-talk round.
