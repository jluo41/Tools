# MISQ Playbook

## Overview

MIS Quarterly is the flagship journal of the Association for Information Systems (AIS). It accepts the broadest range of methodologies of any UTD24 IS journal and places the highest premium on theoretical contribution.

MISQ does NOT mean "easier" — it means different. The bar for theoretical novelty is high, and the review process is demanding. Average time to first decision: 4–6 months. Average R&R cycles: 2–3.

## When MISQ Is the Right Venue

MISQ is the right target when the paper:
- Builds new theory or meaningfully extends existing IS theory
- Uses design science research to create and evaluate an IT artifact with theoretical grounding
- Employs interpretive or qualitative methods with theoretical insight
- Has a contribution to IS research that would be recognized as significant across multiple IS sub-communities
- Challenges or refines a foundational IS construct (e.g., technology acceptance, IS success, IT value)

MISQ is NOT the right target when:
- The primary contribution is a clean causal identification with minimal theory (lean ISR or MS instead)
- The paper is a narrow empirical replication in a new context without theoretical novelty
- The contribution is purely practical with no IS theory development

## Article Types

MISQ publishes five article types. Choosing the wrong type is a common early-stage mistake.

**Research Article**
Standard full-length empirical or design paper. Requires theory + method + contribution. Typical length: 40–50 pages. This is the default for most submissions.

**Research Note**
Shorter contribution (15–25 pages) that addresses a focused question. Appropriate for: replication with extension, one clean finding that does not warrant full paper length, focused theory test. Not a dumping ground for incomplete work.

**Theory and Review**
Pure conceptual contribution. Must propose new theory, integrate fragmented literature into a coherent framework, or provide a systematic review with clear theoretical synthesis. Reviewers apply the highest theoretical rigor here.

**Research Commentary**
Invited or submitted short commentary on a theoretical debate, methodological controversy, or emerging phenomenon. 5–10 pages. Rarely solicited — contact editor before submitting.

**Introduction to Special Issue**
Invited only.

## What MISQ Reviewers Look For

### Theory

MISQ reviewers will ask: "What is the theoretical contribution?" every time. Prepare a crisp one-paragraph answer.

Strong theoretical contributions at MISQ:
- Identify a construct important to IS that existing theory does not adequately capture
- Specify relationships between constructs with clear causal logic
- Derive testable implications or design principles
- Resolve a theoretical tension or contradiction in existing IS work

Weak theoretical contributions that draw rejection:
- "We apply TAM to a new context" without any modification or boundary condition insight
- Theoretical background section that reviews theories without deriving specific predictions
- "Future research should explore..." used as a substitute for actual theoretical development

### Methodology Pluralism

MISQ accepts: surveys, field experiments, lab experiments, econometrics, qualitative case studies, design science, action research, mixed methods, simulation.

Every method must be executed rigorously by that method's own standards. A qualitative paper is judged by qualitative standards (theoretical sampling, theoretical saturation, thick description). An experiment is judged by experimental standards (manipulation checks, control conditions, power analysis).

Do not mix methods unless you can execute both rigorously. A weak qualitative study plus a weak survey is rejected faster than either alone.

### Design Science Papers at MISQ

MISQ is the premier venue for design science research. Use Hevner et al. (2004) and Gregor & Hevner (2013) as your methodological anchors.

Required for DSRM papers at MISQ:
- Clear problem instance and meta-requirements
- Artifact formalized (not just described)
- Kernel theory that motivates design decisions
- Evaluation: formative and summative, naturalistic or experimental
- Design principles abstracted from artifact (not just implementation notes)
- Utility demonstrated beyond the single instantiation

MISQ reviewers will reject DSRM papers that produce an artifact without theoretical abstraction.

## Framing the Contribution

MISQ editors and reviewers read for impact on the IS discipline. Frame contributions using IS-community language:

- Reference the MISQ Senior Scholars' Basket of 8 if adjacent (MISQ is one of the 8)
- Cite foundational IS papers in your area — if DeLone & McLean, Venkatesh, Orlikowski, or Walsham are relevant, they must appear
- Position explicitly against IS literature, not just management or computer science literature

Opening paragraph recipe:
```
[IS phenomenon that matters] is [scope/magnitude]. Yet [what IS research has not explained]. 
This paper [approach] to [answer what question], contributing [theoretical and/or empirical advance].
```

## Submission Mechanics

- **Submission system**: ScholarOne Manuscripts
- **Abstract**: 150 words maximum
- **Blind review**: strict — remove all author identifiers including acknowledgments
- **Style guide**: AIS style guide for references
- **Cover letter**: required; state article type, confirm no concurrent submission, and include a 2–3 sentence contribution statement
- **Handling editor**: MISQ uses area editors aligned to research domains; submission routes to area editor, then senior editor; check current editorial board for alignment

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. Theoretical contribution unclear or incremental
2. Literature review does not engage IS literature deeply enough
3. Methodology section insufficient for replication or critique
4. Common method bias not addressed (for survey research)
5. Discussion overstates what the findings actually show
6. Missing engagement with directly relevant MISQ papers
7. Design science paper lacks abstracted design principles
8. Research question answered by existing literature (contribution not established)
