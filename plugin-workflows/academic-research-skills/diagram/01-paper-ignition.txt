🔥  Paper Ignition — Experiment x Narrative Convergence
=======================================================

  This diagram summarizes a design discussion about ARS:

  Current ARS has research, writing, review, revision, and integrity
  gates. But the real writing trigger should be earlier and sharper:

      "Do we have a supported claim AND a story worth selling?"

─§ ⚖️ Balance rule ────────────────────────────────────────

  +-------------------------------+------------------------------+
  | Situation                     | Verdict                      |
  +-------------------------------+------------------------------+
  | 🎭 Great narrative             | ❌ Not enough                |
  | 🧪 weak evidence               | story becomes overclaim      |
  +-------------------------------+------------------------------+
  | 🧪 Great results               | ❌ Not enough                |
  | 🎭 weak narrative              | paper becomes dull report    |
  +-------------------------------+------------------------------+
  | 🧪 supported claim             | ✅ Ready to write            |
  | 🎭 sellable story              | paper has force              |
  +-------------------------------+------------------------------+

  The paper is not the experiment report.
  The paper is the fusion of experimental results and narrative force.


─§ 🌳 Narrative decision tree idea ────────────────────────

  Before drafting, the user should choose a story route.

  🎯 Global story angle
     │
     ├─ A. 🕳️ Gap story
     │     "The field cannot explain X yet."
     │
     ├─ B. ⚔️ Debate story
     │     "Two camps disagree; our evidence resolves the tension."
     │
     ├─ C. ⚡ Surprise story
     │     "Everyone expected X, but the result shows Y."
     │
     ├─ D. 🔬 Method story
     │     "The old measurement misses the phenomenon."
     │
     └─ E. 🚨 Urgency story
           "This matters now because the world has changed."


  Then each section gets a narrative job:

  +-------------------+-------------------------------------------+
  | Section           | Possible narrative jobs                   |
  +-------------------+-------------------------------------------+
  | 🚪 Introduction   | create urgency · name gap · stage debate  |
  | 📚 Lit Review     | show camps · expose missing mechanism     |
  | 🔬 Methods        | build trust · justify design · bound claim|
  | 📊 Results        | reveal delta · test claim · show surprise |
  | 💬 Discussion     | interpret meaning · handle objections     |
  | 🎁 Conclusion     | compress contribution into one takeaway   |
  +-------------------+-------------------------------------------+

  The route matters:

       Gap → missing mechanism → method isolates mechanism
           → result reveals mechanism → field implication

       Debate → competing explanations → comparative design
              → evidence favors one → revised theory

       Urgency → policy/practice failure → empirical pattern
               → actionable recommendation → why now


─§ 📜 Evidence contract ───────────────────────────────────

  Probe Line should produce an honest claim boundary.

  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+
  | Field           | Question                                   |
  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+
  | 🎯 Claim         | What exactly can we say?                   |
  | 📊 Support       | Which result supports it?                  |
  | 🧱 Strength      | strong · moderate · weak · contradictory  |
  | 🚫 Cannot claim  | What would be overclaiming?                |
  | ⚠️ Risk          | What would a reviewer attack?              |
  | 🔧 Next fix      | extra analysis · limitation · reframe      |
  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+

  Gold rule:

      🧪 Experiment says what is true enough.
      🎭 Narrative decides why anyone should care.


─§ 🎭 Narrative contract ──────────────────────────────────

  Narrative Line should produce a reader-facing story contract.

  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+
  | Field           | Question                                   |
  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+
  | 🧲 Hook          | Why should the reader care now?            |
  | 🕳️ Gap           | What is missing, broken, or misunderstood? |
  | 🔁 Tension       | What expectation does the paper disturb?   |
  | 🎯 Delta         | What changes after this paper?             |
  | 🧑 Reader        | Who needs to believe this?                 |
  | 🎁 Takeaway      | What one sentence should survive?          |
  +-----------------+--------------------------------------------+

  If the user cannot say the paper's one-sentence message,
  drafting is premature.


─§ 🚦 Paper ignition gate ─────────────────────────────────

  Start writing only when all three are true:

  +----+----------------------+-----------------------------------+
  |    | Gate                 | Pass signal                       |
  +----+----------------------+-----------------------------------+
  | 1️⃣ | 🧪 Evidence readiness | results support the core claim    |
  | 2️⃣ | 🎭 Narrative readiness| story angle has tension + payoff  |
  | 3️⃣ | 🧑 Author readiness   | user wants to sell this story     |
  +----+----------------------+-----------------------------------+

  If any gate fails:

      🧪 weak evidence       → run / redesign experiment
      🎭 weak story          → explore narrative decision tree
      🧑 low author energy   → find sharper angle or stop


─§ 💬 Quotes to preserve ──────────────────────────────────

  "Paper is not the result of writing.
   Paper is the expression that appears after Probe Line
   and Narrative Line converge."

  "Research gives possibility.
   Probe gives truth pressure.
   Narrative gives desire.
   Only when truth and desire meet should writing begin."

  "A good narrative without evidence becomes overclaim.
   Good results without narrative become a dull report."

  "The writing point is not when the topic is chosen.
   It is when the author can say:
   I know why this matters, I know what I can claim,
   and I want to sell this story."

  "The system should not push the user into writing.
   It should help the user reach the point where writing feels inevitable."

  "Before drafting, ask:
   Is this claim supported enough to be honest,
   and exciting enough to be worth reading?"
